A Danish Settlement in Southern India

The Personal Website of Surya Sanjay

6 hours to go on the highway back home. No cellular service. A beating sun making my eyes heavier and heavier by the minute. With so many reasons to doze off, it was a miracle that I was conscious enough to notice a familiar-sounding street sign while driving through the countryside of Tamil Nadu, the southernmost state in India. The text—“tharangambadi”—transported me back to the days when I would surf Wikipedia to learn about Indian history as a middle schooler. My eyes suddenly opened wide: this was one of the few colonial Danish settlements in the Indian subcontinent. I urged the driver to take the detour so we could explore the city.

To my surprise, neither the driver nor my parents knew that the Danish had ever colonized parts of India. So, we all were in awe as we passed by the anachronistic architectural juxtaposition of a centuries-old European arch between two native-style houses. We reached the coast—the village was originally a port—and exited the car to see townsfolk walking through a worn-down plaza with streetlamps that hadn’t been used in decades and a domineering fort...
