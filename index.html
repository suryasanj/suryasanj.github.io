The Personal Website of Surya San

Hello there! I’m Surya Sanjay, and I’m a second-year M. S. student in Michigan Medicine’s Bioinformatics Accelerated Master’s Degree Program (amdp). My curricular focus is biomedical signal and image processing, and my research, mentored by Dr. David Nordsletten, focuses on creating patient-specific digital twins of the aorta. This is the chief vessel that transports oxygenated blood out of the heart.

I completed my B. S. in Biology, Health, & Society, with a minor in Linguistics, also at the University of Michigan. During that time, I involved myself in a molecular biophysics project under Dr. Yu-Ming Huang, as well as historical Dravidian linguistics research This is the study of the origin of a group of related languages mostly concentrated in southern India. One of these, Tamil, is my mother tongue. under Dr. David McAlpin as part of DravLing.

I’m working towards a career as a cardiac surgeon-scientist, a role that combines both practice and research.
