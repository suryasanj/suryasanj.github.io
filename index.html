Hello! I’m Surya. Welcome to my personal website.

Who are you?

My name is Surya Sanjay, and I am from Troy, Michigan.

I’m a second-year master’s student at the University of Michigan (Go Blue!), pursuing a career in academic cardiovascular surgery. I study bioinformatics, and my research uses computational biomechanics tools to model the heart and its many vessels. On the side, I also study South Asian historical linguistics with a focus on verbal morphology. In my free time, I love to hike and run, try new cuisines and cafés, and collect vinyl records.

What’s your track record?

I completed my B. S. in Biology, Health, and Society at the University of Michigan with a minor in Linguistics. During college, I was a member of the Michigan Synthetic Biology Team and the Phi Delta Epsilon Pre-Medical Fraternity. I also helped kickstart a cardiac surgery shadowing program for undergraduate students.

Since 2022, I’ve worked with Dr. David Nordsletten to study how simulating fluid and solid mechanics in the aorta can improve surgical decision making. I use finite element modelling to characterize patient physiological states with the goal of informing how and when to perform a procedure. In 2024, I pitched a startup aimed at identifying the optimal endograft to treat aortic aneurysms, which won $5,000 in funding at the Michigan Surgical Innovation Accelerator Finally, in 2025, I interned at Thubrikar Aortic Valve, where I examined the market and regulatory landscapes of transcatheter aortic valve devices.

Finally, I studied historical linguistics under Dr. David McAlpin, with whom I focused on the evolution of tense morphemes in South Dravidian languages. This project is ongoing, but my current work focuses on Old Tulu and Old Kannada, in particular.
