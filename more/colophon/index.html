Typeface

Roman Serif

For now, the answer is Pliego.

For countless years, I’ve adopted and thrown away many fonts as my use cases became more niche and my taste more specific. I began with Times New Roman, inspired by its seemingly academic look in my eighth-grade English class. Seeing computer science articles for the first time late in high school got me interested in Computer Modern Serif, the default font in LaTeX.

As I began to read and write linguistics papers, I developed a taste for old-style and transitional serif fonts with diacritic support, which remain my favorite to this day. From 2021–2022, I toyed around with Baskerville and Palatino; coincidentally, this is when I set up my first personal website. However, in early 2022, a friend introduced me to Brill, with which I continue to have a love-hate relationship.

Brill is the best linguistics-focused font I have ever laid eyes on. It captures all the diacritics I can think of and then some, all while retaining an old-style charm, functioning in an academic setting, and looking very aesthetic. Maybe too aesthetic. The curves of the italic face are very elegant—and I prefer them visually over any other typeface—but they are too whimsical, in my opinion, for serious writing.

As such, ever since, I’ve been searching for replacements. I started with Linux Libertine, which was seemingly too similar to Times New Roman. Then, I switched to Minion 3, which I used for perhaps 2–3 years. I hadn’t noticed that font before, but now having used it as my flagship, I began to see it everywhere. See Matthew Butterick’s notes on it.

At this point, I had run out of options. I could not find a single good-looking font to replace the previous ones, and I had to make a compromise—diacritic support, or aesthetics. I chose the latter. For a short period of time, I used Arno Pro (having found it in an obscure Polish cardiology journal), just because it looked slightly different from Minion 3 but not so much that it was a large adjustment.

And then I found Pliego. It seems an homage to pre-20th century typefaces while also retaining some novel elements. It has charm. And, it was voted the best new font of 2026 by ???. So, I’ve been using it ever since.
