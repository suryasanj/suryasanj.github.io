Kr̩tigaḷu

Surya Sanjay

This is part of a series on Canarese (Kannada) literature. Purandaradāsa (1470–1564) is known as the father of Carnatic music, the classical music of southern India. He wrote mainly in Kannada, the language of the modern-day state of Karnataka, hence the name given to the style of music. Purandaradāsa wrote many kr̩ti-s, or “compositions,” about the Hindu god Vishnu—praising his acts and qualities, while yearning for his refuge. His lyricisms are unlike many other Carnatic composers that I’ve encountered, making use of plain language in creative ways. Below, I include Purandaradāsa kr̩ti-s that I enjoy listening to.
